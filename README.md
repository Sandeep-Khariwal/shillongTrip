Shillong, the "Scotland of the East," sits at 1,496 metres in the Khasi Hills of Meghalaya. Its colonial-era architecture, pine-scented air, and proximity to some of India's most dramatic landscapes make it a destination unlike any other.

## 1. Umiam Lake (Ward's Lake)

The jewel of Meghalaya, Umiam Lake is a man-made reservoir created in 1965 by damming the Umiam river. At over 220 sq km, the reservoir is surrounded by pine-covered hills that mirror perfectly in the water.

**Getting There:** 12 km from the city center via NH6. Shared taxis and local buses run regularly.

**Best Time:** October to March for misty mornings and cleaner skies.

**Tip:** Rent a pedal boat at the Water Sports Institute — they have kayaks too, if you want a proper workout with your views.

## 2. Elephant Falls

Named by the British for an elephant-shaped rock (destroyed in a 1897 earthquake), Elephant Falls is a three-tiered waterfall hidden inside a lush gorge. The path descends through tropical vegetation to each tier.

**Tip:** Wear rubber-soled shoes. The steps down to the lower tiers are steep and can be extremely slippery after rain.

## 3. Shillong Peak

The highest point in the state at 1,965m, Shillong Peak is a restricted military area that still welcomes tourists with a permit issued at the gate. On clear days, you can see as far as the plains of Bangladesh.

**When to Go:** Early mornings in winter (November–February) offer the clearest panoramic views.

## 4. Laitlum Canyon

The name means "End of Hills" in Khasi, and standing at the edge of this canyon you understand why. Clouds roll in from the Bangladesh plains and fill the valley below while you stand in sunshine above. It's one of those views that makes you feel very small in the best possible way.

## 5. Don Bosco Museum

Often overlooked by tourists rushing to the waterfalls, this seven-floor museum on Mawlai Road is one of the finest tribal culture museums in Asia. The rooftop sky-bridge offers a 360° view of Shillong.

**Entry:** ₹80 adults, ₹40 children. Closed Sundays.

## 6. Mawlynnong — Asia's Cleanest Village

80 km from Shillong, this village has maintained its title as Asia's Cleanest Village since 2003. Bamboo trash bins on every corner, community-maintained paths, and a sky-walk bamboo tower that rises above the treetops.

## 7. Dawki & Umngot River

The Umngot River at Dawki is genuinely one of the world's clearest rivers. Boat rides on transparent water where your vessel appears to levitate above the riverbed are as extraordinary as every photograph suggests.

## 8. Double Decker Living Root Bridge

The piece de resistance of Meghalaya's natural wonders. The Khasi tribes have guided rubber fig tree roots across rivers for centuries, creating living bridges that strengthen with age. The Double Decker bridge near Nongriat requires a steep 3,500-step descent — plan for 4–5 hours round trip.

## 9. Cherrapunji (Sohra)

The wettest place on earth (by annual rainfall) sits 56 km from Shillong. In monsoon, waterfalls erupt from every cliff face. In winter, visibility extends to the distant plains. Nohkalikai Falls, India's tallest plunge waterfall at 340m, is the centrepiece.

## 10. Mawphlang Sacred Forest

A sacred grove maintained by the Khasi tribe for over 200 years. The forest is protected by ancient law — nothing may be removed, not even a fallen leaf. Local guides interpret the ecological and spiritual significance of this remarkable ecosystem.

---

## Planning Your Shillong Trip

**Best Time to Visit:** October to April offers clear skies, waterfalls (post-monsoon), and pleasant temperatures.

**Getting to Shillong:** Fly to Guwahati (100 km away, connected to major Indian cities), then take a shared taxi (₹300–400, 3 hours) or private car.

**Budget:** ₹2,000–3,000/day for mid-range accommodation + meals + one activity.

Shillong rewards slow travellers. Build at least 5–7 days if you want to go beyond the main attractions and discover the quieter corners of the Khasi Hills.

Meghalaya in three days is ambitious but very doable if you plan your mornings carefully. This itinerary covers the state's three most iconic experiences: Shillong's colonial character, Cherrapunji's waterfall drama, and Dawki's surreal clarity.

## Day 1: Shillong — Settle In & Explore

**Morning**
Arrive in Shillong early (take the overnight bus from Guwahati or fly in). Check into your hotel, freshen up, and head straight to Umiam Lake before 8 AM — the morning mist on the water is worth the early alarm.

**Afternoon**
Drive back to the city, grab jadoh (Khasi rice-pork dish) at a local eatery near Bara Bazar, and spend the afternoon at the Don Bosco Museum (closed Sundays) — allow at least 2 hours for the seven floors.

**Evening**
Walk through Police Bazar, Shillong's lively commercial hub. Pick up local Lakadong turmeric, bamboo products, and Khasi shawls at the market. Dinner at Café Shillong Heritage — try the pine-cone coffee.

## Day 2: Cherrapunji — Waterfalls & Caves

**Early Morning**
Depart Shillong by 6:30 AM for Cherrapunji (56 km, ~2 hours via the winding Sohra road). Stop at Laitlum Canyon on the way out — it's spectacular in early morning light.

**Morning**
Reach Nohkalikai Falls viewpoint. At 340m, this is India's tallest plunge waterfall. The water falls into a green pool at the base — the colour is caused by dissolved minerals. Continue to Mawsmai Cave (bring a torch and watch your head — it gets tight in places).

**Afternoon**
Head to Eco Park for the plateau views and Seven Sisters Falls (Nohsngithiang Falls) viewpoint. In monsoon, these seven-segmented falls are thundering walls of white. In winter, they're quieter but the air is crystal clear and the views extend for 100 km.

**Evening**
Drive back to Shillong or book a night in Cherrapunji for sunrise views. Several good homestays operate on the plateau.

## Day 3: Dawki — The Glass River

**Early Morning**
Leave Shillong at 5:30 AM for Dawki (82 km, ~3 hours). You want to be at the river before 9 AM — the water is calmest and clearest in the early morning, and boat queues get long by 11 AM.

**Morning**
Take a 30-minute boat ride on the Umngot river. Look down. The boat appears to float in mid-air. This optical illusion, caused by the river's extraordinary clarity, is as extraordinary in person as in photographs.

Cross the suspension bridge to Bangladesh (you'll need a passport if you plan to cross; most tourists just walk to the midpoint for photographs). Don't miss the small market on the Indian side selling Bangladeshi goods.

**Afternoon**
Drive back via Shnongpdeng village (15 min from Dawki) — a quieter stretch of the Umngot river with camping, cliff jumping, and snorkelling in the clear water.

Return to Shillong by evening for your journey home.

---

## Practical Notes

**Transport:** Hire a private car (₹3,500–5,000/day) for maximum flexibility. For budget travel, shared taxis run between Shillong, Cherrapunji, and Dawki — but timings are unpredictable.

**Accommodation:** Stay in Shillong (most options) or split your nights between Shillong and a Cherrapunji homestay.

**What to Pack:** Layers (it's cooler than you think at elevation), good walking shoes, rain jacket (even in winter, showers happen), and a powerbank.

**Budget Estimate (per person):** ₹5,000–8,000 for 3 nights accommodation + transport + meals.

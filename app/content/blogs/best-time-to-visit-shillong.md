Meghalaya is one of the few Indian states where every season changes the destination completely. The monsoon that drowns much of India transforms Meghalaya into a green paradise with 200+ active waterfalls. The dry winter strips the landscape but brings visibility so clear you can see Bangladesh.

## October – November: Post-Monsoon Peak

The best of all worlds. The landscape is lush from monsoon rains, waterfalls are still flowing at half-power, the air has cleared, and temperatures are perfect (15–22°C). This is Shillong at its most photogenic.

**What's Great:** Everything. Living root bridges have maximum greenery, Dawki river is beginning to clear after monsoon turbidity, skies are blue.

**Crowds:** Building up through November. Book accommodation in advance.

## December – February: Cool and Clear

Shillong's winter draws visitors who want the panoramic views. Shillong Peak and Laitlum Canyon on clear December days can see all the way to the Brahmaputra plains. Temperatures drop to 4–10°C at night — bring warm layers.

**What's Great:** Clearest views, lowest humidity, Umngot river at its most transparent (ideal for Dawki boat rides).

**What's Missing:** Major waterfalls reduce to trickles or dry up entirely.

## March – May: Spring Bloom

The hills turn golden and purple with wildflowers. Temperatures warm to 20–25°C, making walking comfortable. Cherry blossoms bloom in Shillong in November–December and again in March.

**What's Great:** Comfortable temperatures, fewer tourists than peak season, wildflowers.

**Tip:** The Shillong Cherry Blossom Festival usually happens in November.

## June – September: Monsoon — The Waterfall Season

Meghalaya receives some of the heaviest rainfall on earth. Cherrapunji and Mawsynram regularly record world records. Every cliff becomes a waterfall. The Living Root Bridges swell with dramatic water flow. The entire landscape turns an impossible shade of emerald.

**What's Great:** 200+ waterfalls active simultaneously. Rivers are powerful and dramatic. Landscapes are at their most lush.

**What's Challenging:** Roads can wash out, mist limits visibility, outdoor activities are restricted. This is not the time for Dawki (the river is murky brown in monsoon).

**Tip:** Book a driver who knows the roads. Carry a waterproof bag for electronics. The rain doesn't stop — it intensifies.

---

## Quick Summary

| Season | Months | Highlights | Avoid If |
|--------|--------|------------|----------|
| Post-Monsoon | Oct–Nov | Best overall | You hate crowds |
| Winter | Dec–Feb | Crystal views, Dawki | You want waterfalls |
| Spring | Mar–May | Flowers, warmth | - |
| Monsoon | Jun–Sep | Epic waterfalls | You want clear views |

For most first-time visitors, **October to February** is the recommendation. But if you want to see Meghalaya's monsoon waterfalls, plan for July–August with flexible dates and a good local driver.
